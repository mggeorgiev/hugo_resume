    Introduction in the Privacy Policy

    The legal definition of privacy and privacy policy is broad. Any information relating to an identified or identifiable person is considered to be personal data (for the full explanation, see Article 4, point 1 of GDPR).

    Examples of personal information are phone numbers, addresses, financial information, identification numbers, email addresses, and more. In the following lines, we refer to a privacy policy that pertains to the collection and storage of personal data by site users.

    COLLECTION OF PERSONAL DATA

    A number of our activities include the collection and processing of personal data, such as contact acceptance and more.

    Such collection and processing of personal data and their subsequent use should be carried out fairly and lawfully.

    PURPOSE OF COLLECTION

    When personal data are required, it is essential that the data subject (the person whose personal data will be collected, stored, or processed) knows for what purposes the data is being collected. According to Article 5 (1) (b) of the Regulation, personal data:

    “collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes; ...".

    Besides, personal data should be adequate, relevant, and not excessive concerning the goal. They must be kept for a period no longer than the necessary for the purpose, for which they were collected.
